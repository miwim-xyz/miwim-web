# Introduction

## What is Miwim?

Miwim is a decentralized proxy infrastructure powered by DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks). It enables anyone with idle server capacity or home broadband to contribute bandwidth to a global proxy network and earn $MIWIM token rewards in return.

On the demand side, Miwim serves two distinct markets:

- **Miwim Backbone**: A decentralized VPN for everyday consumers — low-latency datacenter relay nodes with one-tap mobile connectivity and monthly subscriptions starting at $8/month.
- **Miwim Edge**: Genuine residential IP proxies for business users — ethically sourced home broadband IPs for e-commerce, data collection, and anti-detection use cases, priced at $1-3/GB.

Both products share the same decentralized supply network, the same application, and the same token economy. The differentiation is in routing logic and pricing — not infrastructure.

## Why Now?

Three converging trends make this the right time to build Miwim:

### 1. DePIN is proven
The DePIN category has grown from concept to $4B+ TVL in under two years. Projects like Helium (wireless), Filecoin (storage), and Grass (bandwidth) have demonstrated that token-incentivized physical infrastructure works at scale. Grass alone achieved a $1B+ valuation with 2M+ active nodes — proving that bandwidth aggregation via DePIN is a viable and valuable model.

### 2. Censorship is accelerating
Over 800 million internet users now live behind government firewalls. China's Great Firewall continues to tighten, with regular crackdowns on VPN services. Russia, Iran, and Vietnam have intensified internet restrictions. Traditional centralized VPN providers are increasingly ineffective — their datacenter IPs are trivially detected and blocked by Deep Packet Inspection (DPI).

### 3. The residential IP market is exploding
Enterprise demand for genuine residential IPs — for e-commerce, ad verification, web scraping, and account management — has created a $1.5B market growing rapidly. But the current market is dominated by a handful of players (Bright Data, Oxylabs) charging $8-15/GB with ethically questionable IP sourcing. DePIN offers a fundamentally better model: providers opt in with informed consent and get paid fairly.
