# The Problem

The centralized proxy and VPN market suffers from three fundamental flaws that cannot be fixed within the existing architecture.

## 1. Trust Crisis

Centralized VPN and proxy providers require users to trust a single entity with their traffic, their data, and their prepaid funds. This trust is routinely violated:

- **Operator disappearances**: In the Chinese proxy market alone, 10+ major operators shut down without warning in 2023-2024, each affecting hundreds of thousands of users who lost prepaid balances.
- **Data breaches**: Centralized providers are honeypots for user data. When compromised, every user is exposed simultaneously.
- **No recourse**: When an operator vanishes or is seized, users have no mechanism to recover funds or migrate their service.

Users have adapted by maintaining 2-3 backup subscriptions simultaneously — a tax on consumers caused entirely by architectural centralization.

## 2. IP Resource Monopoly

The residential IP proxy market is controlled by a handful of companies:

- **Bright Data** (formerly Luminati) controls 72M+ residential IPs and charges **$8-15 per GB**
- **Oxylabs** and **Smartproxy** operate similar models at comparable pricing
- IP sourcing is ethically questionable — many residential IPs are obtained by bundling bandwidth extraction into free mobile apps without clear user consent

This creates a market where:
- Prices are 10-50x higher than they need to be
- Access is restricted to enterprise buyers
- The actual bandwidth providers (home internet users) receive little to no compensation
- Quality is inconsistent because providers don't know they're participating

## 3. Single Point of Failure

Every centralized proxy service has one failure mode: the operator. When that single point fails — whether through government seizure, financial collapse, or deliberate exit — every user loses access simultaneously.

This is not a theoretical risk. It happens regularly:
- Government crackdowns on proxy operators in China, Russia, and Iran
- Server seizures affecting entire user bases overnight
- Payment processor blocks cutting off services without warning

There is no redundancy, no failover, and no continuity. The user experience is binary: it works until it doesn't.
